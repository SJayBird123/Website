Robotics at Maryland, Testudog Team




❮ ❯

Robotics at Maryland is the University of Maryland's premier student-led robotics organization. Our three projects are Qubo, the Autonomous Submarine, Testudog, the Quadruped Dog, and Terry, the Mars Rover.
I joined Qubo in my freshman year, but am now both a part of Testudog and leadership, as the Testudog Business Associate and Vision Lead. As a member of software, I have been assisting our controls subproject with developing our sensing platforms for navigation. Presently, our system is a sensor fusion of IMUs, LiDar, and Stereo Cameras.
We are preparing to compete at the IEEE ICRA Legged Robot Challenges in Vienna, Austria this summer. This challenge requires autonomously navigating an obstacle course with three levels of difficulty.

TOOLS: Python, Linux, CAD, C++, Nvidia Jetson Orin, Oak-D S2 Stereo Camera, Unitree Lidar L2
